How to Transfer and Watch Movies Offline on iOS

March 6, 2026 • 6 min read

Watching movies offline on an iPhone or iPad can be frustrating if you rely only on Apple's default apps. Many video formats require conversion, and traditional transfer methods often depend on cables or iTunes.

Fortunately, the Infuse media player solves this problem. Infuse supports a wide range of formats including MKV, MP4, AVI, and even Blu-ray files, allowing users to store movies directly on their devices and watch them without an internet connection.

This guide explains two of the easiest ways to transfer movies to your iOS device and watch them offline.

Method 1: Wireless Browser Transfer

The fastest way to move movies to your iPhone or iPad is through Infuse's built-in browser transfer feature. This allows files to be uploaded directly from your computer using Wi-Fi.

Wireless browser transfer using Infuse

Step 1: Enable Browser Upload

  • Open Infuse on your iPhone or iPad
  • Tap Settings
  • Select Add Files
  • Choose Via Browser
  • An IP address will appear (example: http://192.168.1.15)

Step 2: Upload the Movie

  • Open a browser on your computer
  • Type the IP address displayed in Infuse
  • Drag and drop your movie file into the browser window
  • Wait for the upload to finish

Method 2: Download from Cloud Storage

If your movie library is already stored online, Infuse can connect to popular cloud services and download the files for offline viewing.

Downloading movies from cloud storage using Infuse

Step 1: Connect Your Cloud Account

  • Open Infuse Settings
  • Tap Add Files
  • Select Saved Shares
  • Choose Google Drive, Dropbox, or OneDrive

Step 2: Download the Movie

  • Browse your folders inside Infuse
  • Select a movie
  • Tap the Download icon
  • The movie will be saved locally for offline playback

Why Many Users Prefer Infuse

Compared to the default Apple TV application, Infuse provides far greater flexibility when managing personal video libraries.

  • Supports MKV, AVI, MP4 and many other formats
  • Automatically downloads subtitles
  • Organizes movies with posters and metadata
  • Allows wireless transfers without cables

Traveler Tip

If you plan to watch movies during flights, use videos encoded with H.264 or HEVC. These formats are optimized for iOS hardware decoding and help preserve battery life during long viewing sessions.

Conclusion

With the Infuse media player, transferring and watching movies offline on iOS devices becomes simple. Wireless uploads and cloud downloads eliminate the need for cables or complicated conversion tools.

For travelers or anyone who prefers offline viewing, Infuse offers one of the most flexible and convenient ways to enjoy personal media libraries on iPhone and iPad.
