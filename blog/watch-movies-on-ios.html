How to Transfer and Watch Movies Offline on iOS

The Infuse Guide — No iTunes Required

If you’ve ever tried to put a high-quality movie on your iPad for a long flight, you know the struggle. Standard Apple apps often require specific formats or clunky cables.

Infuse is the ultimate solution. It is a powerful media player that plays almost any video format including MKV, MP4, AVI, and Blu-ray ISO. It allows you to store movies directly on your device and watch them offline anytime.

Method 1: The "No-Cable" Wireless Transfer (Browser Mode)

This is the fastest way to move movies from your PC or Mac to your iPhone or iPad over your home Wi-Fi.

Wireless browser transfer using Infuse

Step 1: Enable the Infuse Server

  • Open Infuse on your iOS device
  • Tap Settings (Gear Icon) → Add Files
  • Select Via Browser
  • Infuse will show an IP address (example: http://192.168.1.15)
  • Keep this screen open

Step 2: Upload from Your Computer

  • Open a browser (Chrome, Safari, Edge)
  • Type the IP address shown in Infuse
  • Drag and drop your movie files
  • Wait for the upload to finish

Method 2: Syncing from Cloud Storage

If your movies are already stored online (Google Drive, Dropbox, OneDrive), Infuse can download them for offline viewing.

Wireless browser transfer using Infuse

Step 1: Connect Your Cloud Account

  • Open Infuse Settings
  • Tap Add Files
  • Select Saved Shares
  • Choose Google Drive, Dropbox, or OneDrive
  • Log into your account

Step 2: Download Movies

  • Browse your folders in Infuse
  • Select a movie or TV show
  • Tap the Download icon
  • The movie will be saved locally to your device

Comparison: Infuse vs Apple TV App

Infuse

  • Format Support: Plays almost every format including MKV, MP4, AVI, and Blu-ray ISO.
  • Subtitles: Automatically downloads subtitles inside the app.
  • Media Organization: Automatically fetches posters, artwork, and movie metadata.
  • File Transfer: Supports wireless browser transfer without cables.

Apple TV App

  • Format Support: Limited to MP4 and MOV formats.
  • Subtitles: Requires manual subtitle import.
  • Media Organization: Artwork and metadata must be added manually.
  • File Transfer: Requires Finder or iTunes for transferring movies.

How to Manage Your Offline Storage

  • View Downloads: Open the Files tab in Infuse
  • Delete Movies: Swipe left on a file and tap Delete
  • Check Storage: Go to Settings → General → iPhone Storage

Traveler Tip

If you are watching high-resolution 4K files, Infuse can drain your battery faster. For the longest battery life during flights, use videos encoded in H.264 or HEVC, since iOS hardware decoding handles these formats more efficiently.
